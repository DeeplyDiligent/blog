My Life On Kaizen

Aug 2, 2018

If you recall, a few weeks ago I discussed the benefits of incremental improvement, based on the belief that fear is much harder to overcome when making a monumental change, compared to a small, purposeful manipulation. We discussed how we can use this in our own lives, with an example about work and family issues. Today, and probably from here on out, I will be writing about my own life and discuss the implications of doing Kaizen to fuel my self-improvement drive.

One Small Step

What one small step did I take today, that can, in turn, create monumental changes in my life?

Today was Thursday. It has always been one of my goals to have one day where I focus on applying for work, fixing up my resume and other projects, rather than studying for uni. Of course I still have classes, but today was the day that I planned to do that. Although a lot of today was not put to the best of use, here is what I have done so far:

  • Applied for a position at the Big 4
  • Started to add metrics and quantitative data to my resume
  • Developed the front-end for the database system

I believe that this step puts me in the correct direction because a purposeful activity such as setting a day aside for this work allows me to get serious about applying for future roles. It also means I start thinking about how to get better at it each week, leading to further inspiration and motivation.

For now though, the small step has not left me very stressed or confused, so I am completely happy with it.
