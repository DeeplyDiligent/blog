Staring at Our Phones

Jul 30, 2018

We all do it. Day or Night. School or Work. We are constantly staring at a screen. In this series, I want to explore the consequences of this widespread phenomenon. I would also like to know how to best communicate in a thumb tapping world. So, how long do you spend staring at your phone?

The Like Switch

In his book, The Like Switch, Jack Schawfer explores the concept of phones and human communication. In a lecture, he instructs students to communicate face to face about any topic that interests them. The students are unable to utter anything meaningful. It takes a lot of both verbal and non-verbal communication to establish a strong connection and they simply lacked the skills to do this. Then came the phones. The students were asked to text each other instead of talking. Out came the funny, weird and charismatic personality hidden behind a wall of face to face communication. Why does this happen? We will explore this question and more in the next blog post.
